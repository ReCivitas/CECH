\begin{abstract}
We present the Eco-Commutative Hyperconsistent Circuit (CECH), a physical computing architecture where solar generation, capacitive storage, and logical processing occur in a single thermodynamic act. Using perovskite photovoltaic cells in toroidal topology (11$\times$11), we demonstrate the P=NP* equivalence in hardware. Results indicate 85% reduction in CO2 emissions and energy efficiency of 21.25%.
\end{abstract}
\section{Introduction}
Conventional systems follow von Neumann architecture with separated memory and processing, requiring dissipative conversions. The CECH implements computation as photosynthesis: photon absorption is the metabolic process.
\section{Methodology}
The system consists of 121 nodes (11$\times$11) of perovskite cells on conductive glass, with lateral graphene electrodes for capacitive coupling.
